▾ bio ▾

Anupam has worked on atomistic simulations of extreme condition of matter and he has submitted his PhD thesis at Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, India, in June 2017. Prior to that, he earned his B.Sc and M.Sc degree in physics from The University of Burdwan, India in 2008 and 2010, respectively. His broad area of interest is atomic-scale study of plastic deformation and electronic structure calculation of various nano-structured materials.

Ali Khourshaei Shargh

PhD Student

Mechanical Engineering

project: Mechanics of ultrathin SiN porous membranes

Hopeman 211

akhoursh@ur.rochester.edu

▾ bio ▾

Ali joined ACMML in September 2017 as a PhD Student in Mechanical Engineering. He holds a B.Sc. degree from K. N. Toosi University of Technology and an M.Sc. degree from Sharif University of Technology, all in Mechanical engineering. As an M.Sc. student, he worked with Computational Nano/Bio-mechanics group on cell membrane. For his M.Sc. thesis, Ali developed multi-scale model of the viscoelastic behavior of the cell membrane. He is currently working on the failure mechanisms of ultrathin Nanoporous membranes. His research interests include molecular dynamics simulation, Multi-scale modeling, and finite element analysis of microstructures.

Mechanics of ultrathin SiN porous membranes
